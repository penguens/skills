## examples

each example follows the voice pillars: simple, helpful, human, magic (when appropriate).
notice how each "good" version:
1. names the specific problem or action
2. gives a clear next step or outcome
3. sounds like a person talking, not a corporation
4. avoids filler, hype, and cuteness-at-the-wrong-moment

### generic to on-brand

bad:
```text
unlock your full potential with our revolutionary platform.
```

good:
```text
turn scattered work into a clear next step.
```

why:
- the good version is specific
- it avoids hype
- it gives the reader a practical outcome

### error message

bad:
```text
oopsie. we hit a tiny snag.
```

good:
```text
we could not upload this file. check your connection and try again.
```

why:
- the good version names the problem
- it gives a recovery path
- it avoids cuteness in a frustrating moment

### empty state

bad:
```text
nothing to see here.
```

good:
```text
no reports yet. create your first report to start tracking progress.
```

why:
- the good version explains what is missing
- it gives the next action
- it stays simple and helpful

### landing page headline

bad:
```text
the all-in-one solution for modern teams.
```

good:
```text
one place to see what changed, what matters, and what to do next.
```

why:
- the good version is concrete
- it creates a small story
- it avoids generic category language
