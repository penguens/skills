# tone by context

adapt the tone without changing the voice.

## onboarding

lead with confidence and momentum.

use:
- what is happening
- why it matters
- what to do next

avoid:
- long explanations before the user acts
- vague welcome copy
- excessive excitement

## empty states

make the empty state useful.

use:
- what is missing
- why it is empty
- the next useful action

example:
```text
no projects yet. create one to start organizing your work.
```

## errors

be calm, specific, and recovery-focused.

use:
- what failed
- whether anything was lost
- how to fix it

avoid wit here unless the issue is harmless.

bad:
```text
oops, something went sideways.
```

good:
```text
we could not save your changes. check your connection and try again.
```

## security, privacy, legal, and billing

prioritize clarity and trust.

use:
- plain terms
- direct consequences
- exact actions
- no jokes

avoid:
- cute language
- vague reassurance
- buried conditions
- unnecessary personality

## marketing and landing pages

start strong and stick to one clear message per section.

use:
- concrete outcomes
- audience-aware benefits
- sharp headlines
- proof, not hype

avoid:
- generic saas claims
- exaggerated promises
- stacked buzzwords
- multiple messages in one section

## social and campaign copy

be lean, vivid, and channel-aware.

use:
- one idea
- a strong opening
- language that sounds spoken
- a clear reason to care

avoid:
- copy-pasting messages between channels
- overpolished corporate phrasing
- posts that only announce, but do not invite

## product documentation

make complexity feel navigable.

use:
- plain definitions
- ordered steps
- examples close to real usage
- direct warnings when needed

avoid:
- assuming expert context
- burying prerequisites
- long paragraphs with mixed actions
